### Sweat Equity
**Sweat equity** refers to the shares of a company issued to its directors or employees at a discount or for consideration other than cash. This is essentially a reward for their "sweat"—the value they have added to the company through their intellectual property, know-how, or technical expertise. It aligns the interests of the employees with the long-term growth of the company, as they become shareholders.

---

### Price-to-Earnings (PE) Ratio
The **PE Ratio** is a valuation metric that measures the current share price of a company relative to its earnings per share (EPS).
*   **Formula:** `Market Price per Share / Earnings per Share`
*   **Meaning:** It tells you how much an investor is willing to pay for every ₹1 of profit the company generates. A high PE suggests investors expect high future growth, while a low PE might suggest the stock is undervalued or the company is facing challenges.

#### What is a "Good" PE for Indian IT Stocks?
There is no single "good" number, as it depends on the company's growth stage and market cycle. However, for the Indian IT sector:

1.  **Historical Context:** Large-cap IT giants (like TCS or Infosys) typically trade in a PE range of **25x to 35x**. Mid-cap IT companies often trade at higher multiples if they are growing faster, or lower if they are considered "value" plays.
2.  **Growth vs. Valuation:** A "good" PE is relative to the company's **Earnings Growth Rate**. If a company has a PE of 30 but is growing its profits at 25% annually, it might be considered reasonably valued.
3.  **Sector Sentiment:** During periods of high demand for digital transformation, IT stocks often command premium PEs (sometimes 40x+). During economic slowdowns or when growth plateaus, these multiples tend to contract toward 20x–25x.

**Key Takeaway:** Always compare a company's PE against its own historical average and its direct peers rather than looking at an absolute number.
